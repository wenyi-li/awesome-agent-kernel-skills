## Summary

Extends `benchmarks/tune_ex2_emu.py` with two improvements needed for tuning the hd256 forward kernel on B200 (SM100).

### hd256 sweep support

The hd256 softmax loop steps `k` by 2 (rather than 1), so `k % freq` only takes even values. As a result, `ex2_emu_res` cannot be inferred from `freq` alone — it must be swept independently to explore distinct hardware-vs-emulated `exp2` ratios.

Changes:
- Phase 1 adds `ex2_emu_res` as a third sweep axis for hd256 keys (on top of the existing `ex2_emu_freq × ex2_emu_start_frg` grid).
- Phase 2 (register count sweep) is skipped for hd256: `num_regs_other` is fixed at 32 in the hd256 kernel, and the 512-budget formula used for smaller head dims does not apply.

### GPU clock lock/unlock

Benchmark variability from frequency scaling can obscure small performance differences during tuning. The script now locks clocks to the hardware maximum at startup and restores them on exit.

Changes:
- `setup_clocks(do_lock)` locks GPU clocks at startup and registers `unlock_clocks()` via `atexit`, so clocks are restored even if the script is interrupted mid-sweep.
