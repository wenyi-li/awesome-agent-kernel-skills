## Summary

FlashInfer PR #2927 introduced a shared `tile_fits_smem(sm, m, n, k_elem, config, stages=2)` predicate for CUTLASS-backed GEMM candidate filtering. The helper encodes per-architecture shared-memory budgets and is used by both the SM12x path and the Ampere mainloop path.

For Ampere work, the important lesson is that A100-oriented tile choices must not be promoted blindly to every SM 8x GPU. GA10x/SM86-class devices have a smaller opt-in shared-memory budget than A100, so tile candidates and stage counts need an architecture-aware SMEM predicate before autotuning or JIT compilation.

## Kernel-KBS Lessons

- Filter GEMM tile candidates with an explicit `(sm, M, N, K, stages)` shared-memory predicate before launching tactic probes.
- Treat `sm80` and `sm86` as separate tuning targets when shared-memory residency is close to the limit.
- Include pipeline stage count in the SMEM check; a tile that fits at two stages may fail at three or four stages.
- Drain CUDA errors after failed tactic probes before graph capture or subsequent candidates. Otherwise one unsupported tactic can surface later as a misleading sticky async error.
- Aggregate skipped-tactic logging for production autotuners; detailed per-tactic information belongs at debug level.
